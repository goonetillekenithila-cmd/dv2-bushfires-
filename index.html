A continent that burns

Where Australians pay for fire - a story of disaster recovery, 2009 to 2026

Every bushfire in Australia leaves a financial scar. When fire damages homes, businesses, and infrastructure, the federal government activates Disaster Recovery Funding Arrangements to help communities rebuild. This is a map of who has paid the price.

01 - The Opening

Every Australian community hit by bushfire

Each shaded state shows the total number of bushfire activations of federal disaster recovery funding since 2009. New South Wales alone accounts for nearly half of all activations.

02 - When bushfires strike

The seasonality and rhythm of fire

Bushfire activations cluster in the southern summer - October through February. Years like 2012, 2019, 2023, and 2025 saw spikes far beyond the baseline; recent years suggest the season may be intensifying.

03 - Where bushfires strike

The geography of Australian bushfire

Bushfire is not evenly shared across Australia. New South Wales bears the heaviest burden by a wide margin, with Queensland's exposure growing sharply in recent years. The small states see occasional but rarely concentrated activity.

04 - The cost

The price communities pay

Disaster recovery funding has four tiers - from immediate personal hardship payments (Cat A) to exceptional recovery measures for catastrophic events (Cat D). Some local government areas have been activated more than a dozen times since 2009.

The ten hardest-hit local government areas

How often each recovery category is activated

A single activation can include multiple categories - Cat A and Cat B together cover the vast majority of activations, while Cat C and D recovery measures are reserved for the most severe events.

Severity of activations by state

Of the activations classified in the government data, A/B indicates personal hardship and essential works, while C/D indicates exceptional recovery for catastrophic events. Roughly half of all bushfire activation records do not have a severity classification in the source data and are not shown here.
